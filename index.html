Supreme Court Clarifies Powers of Higher Courts in Examining Jurisdictional Facts

Published on December 2, 2024 | Category: Civil Procedure

In a significant clarification that impacts civil litigation across India, the Supreme Court has recently established that higher courts maintain the authority to examine jurisdictional facts in cases, even when the trial court may not have explicitly framed an issue regarding maintainability. This ruling comes with the important caveat that such examination should not require the introduction of new facts or evidence at the appellate stage.

This clarification brings greater clarity to the scope of higher courts' powers while ensuring that fundamental jurisdictional issues are not overlooked merely due to procedural oversights at the trial court level.

Practical Implications

Example 1: Consider a property dispute case where the question of territorial jurisdiction wasn't raised in the trial court. Under this ruling, if clear documentary evidence already on record shows the property is outside the court's jurisdiction, a higher court can examine this jurisdictional fact without requiring new evidence.

Example 2: In a civil suit regarding a contract dispute, if the basic document showing the court's jurisdiction (like the agreement with jurisdiction clause) is already part of the trial court record, the higher court can examine this jurisdictional aspect even if it wasn't specifically contested earlier.

This judgment strikes a balance between procedural efficiency and jurisdictional accuracy, ensuring that courts can address fundamental jurisdictional issues while preventing the introduction of new disputed facts at later stages of litigation.
